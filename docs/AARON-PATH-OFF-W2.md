## 2. The math — what it takes to replace W-2

Aaron's W-2: $120,000/year = **$10,000/month gross**.

### 2a. Current targeted comp (from Master Planner)

- Base: $3,000/month
- Commission target: 7% of margin per deal
- At 10 deals/month: $3,000 + (10 × $17,000 × 7%) = $3,000 + $11,900 = **$14,900/month**

That actually exceeds W-2 by $4,900/month — IF and only if 10 deals/month is the reliable run rate.

### 2b. The reliability problem

10 deals this month does not equal 10 deals every month. Master Planner cites cold call as "1 in 100 leads to contract, 9+ month timeline" — meaning current production is the result of 12+ months of pipeline build, and any month could regress. A spike-then-crash quitting pattern is the worst outcome.

### 2c. What "safe to quit" actually means

Safe to quit = the run rate has demonstrated **consistency** AND has a **buffer** above W-2 income to absorb down months without forcing a return-to-W-2 conversation.

Recommended buffer: **30%+ above W-2 monthly equivalent.** That's $13,000/month minimum sustained.

## 3. Recommended trigger

**Aaron quits W-2 the first calendar month immediately following the month in which ALL of the following are true:**

1. **13+ closed contracts in the trigger month.** (Provides $14,900–$17,280 monthly comp at 7% commission, well above $13k buffer.)
2. **11+ closed contracts in EACH of the prior 2 calendar months.** (Confirms sustained run rate, not a single-month spike.)
3. **2+ niche-attributable contracts in the trailing 60 days.** (Validates that the niche-sequential investment earned its keep — if all contracts came from bulk, the new mail/ISA/door-knocker spend didn't move the needle and we should debug before quitting.)

### 3a. Why a 3-condition trigger and not just "13 contracts"

Single-condition triggers fail because real estate is volatile. A great month followed by quitting followed by two slow months = catastrophe. The 3-condition trigger eliminates:

- **Spike risk:** condition (2) requires 90 days of 11+/mo, not one big month
- **Bulk-only risk:** condition (3) requires the niche play to have proven itself, so the post-quit growth thesis is real
- **Lottery risk:** 11/11/13 across 3 months is statistically rare unless the model is genuinely working

### 3b. Why 13 in the trigger month and not higher

13 contracts × 7% × $17k margin = $15,470 commission, plus $3k base = **$18,470/month**. That's 85% buffer above $10k W-2 — covers the inevitable down month without panic. Pushing the trigger to 15 contracts adds delay without much additional safety; 11 contracts gives only a 30% buffer which is too thin.

### 3c. Why 2 niche-attributable contracts in 60 days

Two is the smallest number that's not statistical noise. One could be coincidence. Two over 60 days = evidence of repeatable mechanism. This protects against the failure mode where bulk happens to spike for unrelated reasons (seasonal, lead-list quality variance) and Aaron quits believing the niche play worked when it didn't.

## 5. The bridge plan — how Aaron survives 6–10 months until trigger

Aaron continues W-2 + nights/weekends WHO work. The W-2 covers personal life unchanged. Two design choices to discuss with Travis:

### 5a. Reinvestment of Aaron's deferred commission

**Proposal:** During the bridge period, 100% of commission Aaron WOULD have earned (at the agreed 7% rate) reinvests into the business as marketing / SiftStack capital. Tracked and credited but not paid.

**Why:** Funds Phase 2 mail scaling ($5k/mo) + Phase 3 second-ISA hire WITHOUT requiring new partner capital injection from either Aaron or Travis. Treats Aaron's deferred comp as the war chest that funds the very growth that gets him off W-2.

**Travis's stake:** He doesn't have to put more in. Aaron's labor + deferred comp = the entire investment vehicle.

**Counter-perspective Travis may raise:** This means Aaron is "investing" his commission while drawing nothing, which may feel like an asymmetry going the other direction. Worth surfacing — could be balanced by adjusting commission rate post-trigger (Section 6).

### 5b. Base-rate handling during bridge

**Proposal:** $3k base accrues but does not pay during bridge period. Accrued base = a credit to be paid out from the first 6 months of post-trigger income, OR converted to additional equity (would require partnership agreement amendment).

This makes the bridge feel less like "free labor" and more like "deferred compensation with a clear payback mechanism."

## 6. Post-trigger compensation — the conversation Travis needs to be ready for

When Aaron quits W-2, the partnership needs to have agreed in advance what changes. Don't let this be a Day-1-post-quit negotiation.

### 6a. Commission rate adjustment

**Proposal:** Move from 7% → 8% commission on Aaron's attributable closes, effective the trigger month. Rationale: post-W-2 Aaron's contribution to marketing/sales/SiftStack roughly doubles in hours; the 1% bump partially compensates for that productivity step-change without requiring an equity restructure.

At 13 deals/mo and 8% commission: $3,000 base + $17,680 commission = **$20,680/mo** (vs $14,900 at 7%). Strong buffer over W-2 replacement.

### 6b. Equity unchanged

**Proposal:** 50/50 split stays 50/50. The commission rate adjustment is the lever that compensates for productivity step-change; equity remains as-is to preserve partnership symmetry.

### 6c. Drawing structure post-trigger

**Proposal:** Aaron's draw structure post-trigger:
- $3,000/month base (unchanged from Master Planner target)
- 8% commission paid on settlement of each closed deal in his attribution
- Quarterly review of base + commission rate based on prior quarter's actuals

Simple, transparent, no surprises.

## 7. Conservative-bias safety net

Aaron has flagged he's the more risk-averse of the two partners. The trigger is a **floor for "now you can quit," not a ceiling on "you must quit."** Built-in safety extensions:

### 7a. The 1-month-extra rule

Even if all 3 trigger conditions hit, Aaron may extend W-2 by 1 additional month if any of the following are true the same month:

- Lender pushback on a key in-flight deal (closes at risk)
- DialForce ISA reliability dropping (>1 missed week of cadence in trigger month)
- Mike or Danielle citing capacity issues that suggest the run rate isn't sustainable
- Construction side (Travis) flagging inability to handle deal volume
- Personal financial situation changed (unexpected expense, debt paydown plan disrupted)

**Cost of the extra month: 1 month of pure-WHO income.**
**Cost of quitting too early: potentially 6+ months of forced return-to-W-2 stress.**

The math is heavily asymmetric in favor of patience.

### 7b. Reverse-trigger (post-quit safety)

If Aaron has quit and run rate drops below 8 contracts/month for 2 consecutive months, **don't immediately return to W-2**. First sequence:

1. Diagnose: bulk problem? niche problem? construction-capacity bottleneck? personnel issue?
2. Pause Phase 3 expansion if active
3. Reduce non-essential marketing spend
4. Use 3 months of business cash reserves (target: 3 months operating expenses in reserve before quit)

If after 3 months of intervention contracts haven't recovered to 10+/mo: re-evaluate. Returning to a W-2 is on the table but not the first move.

## 9. What kills this plan (the things that should make Aaron NOT quit)

Be honest with yourself about these. They're the most likely failure modes:

- **Niche play doesn't beat bulk.** If 60 days of mail + ISA produces 0 niche-attributable contracts, the thesis is wrong. Do not quit on bulk-only contracts at 11/mo — bulk has proven volatility risk.
- **Construction side becomes the bottleneck.** Hitting 13 contracts/mo means nothing if Travis can't move the inventory. Pre-quit conversation must include construction-capacity readiness.
- **DataSift partnership ends.** If the free DataSift tier disappears, $0 data cost becomes ~$1–2k/mo. Re-evaluate trigger math.
- **DialForce relationship ends.** If DialForce pulls the free callers, $5k/mo of cold-call cost reappears. Re-evaluate trigger math.
- **Personal debt situation changes.** If personal debt payments shift materially, the $13k/mo buffer threshold changes. Re-run the math, don't assume the trigger is static.
