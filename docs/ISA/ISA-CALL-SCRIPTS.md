# ISA Call Scripts — Help-Framed Openers + Objection Branches

**Audience:** Kareem.
**Purpose:** Exact phone scripts for every preset Kareem dials. Print this. Have it open during every call. Read it until it's automatic, then make it sound like it isn't.

**Read [ISA-SALES-FRAMEWORK.md](ISA-SALES-FRAMEWORK.md) first.** These scripts are the framework operationalized. None of them work without the framework underneath.

---

## How to use this document

Every script follows the same shape:

1. **Opening TARP** (Time / Agenda / Result / Permission to say no)
2. **Help-frame opener** (the "four options" sentence — the load-bearing line)
3. **Bruise acknowledgment** (named calmly, not pressed on)
4. **UMBC capture path** (what to listen for, what to ask)
5. **Branches by distress state** (which of the 8 psychological states they're showing)
6. **Closing TARP** (matches whatever Danielle's call will open with)
7. **Voicemail variant** (every dial that misses gets a tested VM)

# Section 1 — Lis Pendens (Foreclosure Just Filed)

**Situation:** Foreclosure case just filed in Common Pleas Court. Sheriff sale is 4-12 weeks away. Homeowner is in early-stage panic, often in **denial (state #1.2)** or **shame (state #1.1)**. They have the most time and most options of any distress lead.

**Tone:** Calm advisor. Not urgent. "You have time and options" is the whole posture.

## 1.1 LP Phone Opener

> "Hi, is this [first_name]? Hi [first_name], my name is Kareem, I'm with Wright Home Offer here in [county] County.
>
> I know this is a little out of the blue — I'm calling because the foreclosure case on [property_address] was just filed, and I work with folks in your spot every week. Quick promise: this'll take about five minutes, I'll walk through three or four options that usually exist in your situation, and at the end you can decide whether any of them are worth a deeper conversation. If none of them are the right fit, that's perfectly fine — you won't hurt my feelings.
>
> Got five minutes?"

**What's happening in this opener:**
- Confirms the right person (always)
- Local Ohio buyer (disarms scammer pattern)
- Acknowledges the bruise calmly: "the foreclosure case was just filed" — no judgment, no urgency
- TARP: 5 min, four options, decision on whether to keep going, permission to say no
- Ends with a yes/no question — wait in silence

## 1.2 If they say "yes, go ahead"

> "Appreciate it. So when a foreclosure case is filed in [county], folks usually have one of four paths from here, depending on the situation:
>
> One — if you've got equity and want to keep the house, there are bankruptcy attorneys who can buy you time and sometimes restructure the debt. Free intro if you want.
>
> Two — if your income's recovered or recovering, loan modification specialists can sometimes get the bank to redo the terms. Different from the bank doing it themselves — it's a third party negotiating for you.
>
> Three — there's a free HUD-certified counseling service, government-funded, no sales pitch, just neutral guidance.
>
> And four — if it's the right fit, my partners and I sometimes buy houses for cash, as-is, close in 7-14 days, no agent fees, no inspections. That's the option I get paid on, and I'm telling you about it last on purpose. The other three I share because folks ask me about them every day.
>
> Which of those — if any — sounds worth digging into for your situation?"

**What's happening:**
- Four options laid out, cash-sale option **last and labeled honestly**
- "I get paid on this one" — radical transparency disarms the suspicion state
- Open-ended "which sounds worth digging into" — they pick the path. We don't push them onto one.

## 1.3 UMBC Listening Path (during their answer)

As they talk, listen for:

- **U (Urgency):** Has a sheriff sale date been set yet? Are they current on payments anywhere else? Any other deadlines?
- **M (Motivation):** Why did this happen? Job loss? Health? Divorce? Death in family? Press with Socratic follow-ups, **gently** — ("Tell me more about that…" / "How long have you been dealing with that?" / "How does that feel day to day?"). The bruise is exposed; do not press hard.
- **B (Ballpark):** "If you did go the cash-sale route, what's a number that would feel like the situation got resolved? Not asking what you'd accept — asking what would feel like the right outcome."
- **C (Condition):** "Is anyone living there now? Anything I should know about the property — roof, foundation, plumbing, anything you've been wanting to fix but couldn't?"

## 1.4 Branches by distress state

### If they show **Denial (#1.2)** — "the bank's working with me"

> "That's good — keep that going. The folks I work with who land in the best spot usually do two things at once: they keep the bank conversation going AND they have a backup option mapped out. That way if the bank's timeline slips, they're not scrambling. Want me to walk through what the backup usually looks like, just so it's in your pocket?"

**Don't argue. Parallel-track.** Get them to the four options without making them admit the bank's stalling.

### If they show **Shame (#1.1)** — clipped, defensive, fast hangup attempts

> "Hey, before we go any further — I want to be straight with you. Foreclosure happens to thousands of Ohio homeowners every year. Job changes, medical stuff, divorce, family situations. The only thing that separates the people who land okay from the people who don't is whether somebody walks them through their actual options. There's no judgment from my end. If you give me five minutes, I'll lay out the four paths and you can hang up — no pressure, no follow-up call, nothing."

**Lower tonality. Slower words. Make the call feel safe.**

### If they show **Suspicion-of-Vultures (#1.4)** — "Who is this? Where'd you get my number? You a wholesaler?"

> "Totally fair question. The answer is public records — when a foreclosure case is filed in [county] Common Pleas, it's part of the public docket. I'm a local Ohio buyer, Wright Home Offer, based in [city], and I'm not a wholesaler — I close on my own contracts when I do close. I'm calling because I work in this every day, and a lot of folks I talk to don't realize they've got more options than they think. If you want, I can text you our address and license info before we go any further. Or we can keep going for a couple minutes and you can decide."

**Transparency is the only move. Don't be charming. Be informational.**

### If they show **Decision Fatigue (#1.5)** — "I can't deal with this right now"

> "Totally get it. You've probably been making hard calls for weeks. Here's what I'd offer: I'm not asking you to decide anything today. I'm offering you a five-minute call where I lay out four options. You don't have to pick one. You just have to know they exist. When the bandwidth opens up later, you'll have the map. Sound okay?"

**Reduce cognitive load. One small ask.**

### If they show **Magical Thinking (#1.8)** — "My brother's lending me the money"

> "That's great — I hope that comes through. While you're waiting on that, do you want to have the backup option mapped out so you don't have to scramble if the timing slips? Just five minutes, no pressure."

**Don't kill the hope. Parallel it.**

## 1.5 LP Closing TARP (if qualified — handoff to Danielle)

> "Okay [first_name], here's what I'm going to do. Based on what you've shared, I think the right next step is a conversation with Danielle. She's our home-buying specialist, she's the one who can actually run the numbers and tell you what we'd offer. She'll spend about ten minutes on the phone with you — she'll ask a few more questions about the property condition, walk you through how we calculate offers, and by the end you'll know what we'd put on the table. You don't have to decide anything on that call either — same deal as today, no pressure, no obligation.
>
> Does [day] at [time] work, or would [backup day] at [time] be better?"

After they pick:

> "Got it — Danielle will call you [day] at [time]. Just so you know what to expect: about ten minutes, she'll go through the property condition and the offer numbers, and by the end you'll know exactly where you stand. If you decide it's not the right fit, that's totally fine. So [day] at [time]. Let me say that one more time so we're on the same page — [day] at [time]. I'll text you a confirmation right after we hang up."

**State the callback time three times. This is the layer-anchoring rule. Ghost rate plummets when this is done correctly.**

## 1.6 LP Closing TARP (if not yet — recycle)

> "Okay [first_name], it doesn't sound like today's the day for any of these options, and that's totally fine. Here's what I'd ask: would it be okay if I reached back out in a couple weeks to see how things are going? No pressure, no automatic calls — I'd just want to make sure you've got the four options in your back pocket if anything shifts."

If yes → tag `callback_2weeks` in DataSift. If no → Verify The No → re-status. **Never tag Dead.**

## 1.7 LP Voicemail (15 seconds, tested)

> "Hi [first_name], this is Kareem with Wright Home Offer in [county] County. I'm a local buyer and I'm calling about a few options that usually exist after a foreclosure case is filed — not all of them require selling. My number's [number]. Again, [number]. No rush, no pressure — just wanted to make sure you had the options in your pocket. Take care."

# Section 2 — Sheriff Sale Scheduled

**Situation:** Sale date is on the calendar. Days-to-sale dictates urgency. **Time pressure is the dominant force in every conversation.** Most prospects here are in **denial (#1.2)**, **decision fatigue (#1.5)**, or **learned helplessness (#1.3)**.

**Tone:** Direct, urgent, pragmatic. **Never panicked.** Confidence: "I can close before the sale, here's how."

## 2.1 SS Phone Opener (>21 days to sale)

> "Hi, is this [first_name]? Kareem with Wright Home Offer in [county] County. I'm calling because your property at [property_address] is set for sheriff sale on [auction_date] — and I want to make sure you know your options before that date hits. Five minutes, three or four paths, you decide if any of them fit. If none do, that's fine — you won't hurt my feelings.
>
> Got five?"

## 2.2 SS Phone Opener (≤21 days to sale — URGENCY MODE)

> "Hi, is this [first_name]? Kareem with Wright Home Offer in [county]. I'm calling about [property_address] — the sheriff sale's scheduled for [auction_date], so we've got [X] days. I'm not calling to pressure you, I'm calling because there are still some real options on the table before that date — and they get fewer every day. Five minutes, four options, you tell me if any fit. Got five?"

**Even in urgency mode, the opener is calm. Urgency is in the math, not the voice.**

## 2.3 SS Four Options (the menu shifts at this stage)

> "When a sheriff sale is on the calendar, folks usually have one of four paths:
>
> One — emergency Chapter 13 bankruptcy filing. An attorney can sometimes file the day before the sale and stop it. It's a real path if you've got equity and you want to fight to keep the house.
>
> Two — short payoff or short sale negotiation with the bank. Sometimes works, takes time, depends on the loan servicer.
>
> Three — cash sale to us before the sale date. We close in 7-10 days, pay off the bank, you walk away with whatever's left after the payoff. Stops the foreclosure showing as completed on your record. This is the path my partners and I get paid on, and I'm telling you about it third on purpose.
>
> And four — even after the sale, in Ohio you usually have a redemption window. Most people don't know this. We can talk about that if the sale already feels inevitable.
>
> Which of those — if any — sounds worth digging into?"

## 2.4 Common SS Objections

### "I already worked with the bank — they're giving me a forbearance"

> "That's good. Quick reality check though — even with a forbearance, the foreclosure being filed is already on your record once it's public. A forbearance pauses the timeline; it doesn't erase the filing. If the bank's plan works, great. The folks who land best usually run a backup track at the same time. Want me to lay out what the backup looks like? Five minutes, no commitment."

### "I'm fine, the sale isn't going to happen"

> "Got it. Sales do get postponed — that's true. On the off chance things change in the next few weeks, here's my number: [number]. I'm a local Ohio buyer, not a national company. If anything shifts, give me a call. Quick favor — can I check back in a couple weeks just to see where things landed? No pressure either way."

If yes → `callback_2weeks`. **Tag with the actual scheduled date in Smrtphone callback queue.** Sale dates slip; many "I'm fine" calls become deals 30-90 days later.

### "How much would you offer?"

> "Great question. So here's the honest answer — I'm not the partner who sets the number. My job is to get the property details and the situation in front of Danielle, who runs the offers with the partner team. What I can tell you is, our offers are based on what the property's worth fixed up, minus the work it needs, minus a margin so we can actually make money. If you want, the next step is a 10-minute call with Danielle where she runs through the property and gives you the actual number. Want me to set that up?"

**Never quote a number. The 3x3 ballpark rule applies — Kareem extracts, Danielle delivers.**

## 2.5 SS Closing TARP (qualified)

Same shape as LP §1.5 but with stronger time-anchoring on the sheriff sale date:

> "Danielle's call is going to be about ten minutes — property condition, offer math, you'll know where we stand by the end. Given the sale's on [auction_date], let's get her on the phone in the next 24 hours. Does [day] at [time] work, or [backup] at [backup time]?"

State callback time **three times**. Confirm via SMS within 5 minutes of hanging up.

## 2.6 SS Voicemail

> "Hi [first_name], Kareem with Wright Home Offer. Calling about [property_address] before the sheriff sale on [auction_date]. There are usually three or four options before a sale that most folks don't know about — wanted to make sure you had them. My number's [number]. Again, [number]. Take care."

# Section 3 — Probate (Personal Representative / Executor)

**Situation:** Talking to the **executor / Personal Representative** named in court records. The decedent (whose name is in `decedent_name`) is deceased. Most prospects here are in **grief (#1.6)**, **decision fatigue (#1.5)**, or **fear of judgment (#1.7)**.

**Tone:** Empathic. Soft. **Never urgent on Day 1.** We are patient — probate is a 6-9 month process. The runway is on our side.

## 3.1 Probate Phone Opener

> "Hi, is this [first_name]? My name is Kareem, I'm with Wright Home Offer here in [county] County.
>
> First — I want to start by saying I'm sorry for your loss. I saw [decedent_name]'s estate filing and I know losing someone and dealing with the legal side at the same time is overwhelming. I'm not calling to pressure you on anything. I work with families going through probate every week, and there are usually a few options for the property at [property_address] when the family's ready — and the operating word there is *ready*. There is no rush from my end.
>
> Got two minutes? If now's not the time, I can call back in a few weeks instead — totally your call."

**Notice what's different from foreclosure:** explicit condolences first, "ready" emphasized, offer to back off **before** any pitch.

## 3.2 If they're in active grief — back off

If they say something like "we're still figuring things out," "it's only been a few weeks," voice cracks, or they ask to call back later:

> "Of course. I'll back off completely. Here's what I'd offer — let me text you my number and a one-page summary of what the options usually look like when families are ready to look at the property. You can read it whenever, no follow-up call from me unless you reach out first. And if it's helpful in the meantime, I know good probate attorneys and estate-cleanout folks if you ever need a referral. Take care of yourself."

**Tag `probate_grief_back_off` in DataSift. Set 30-day callback. Send the SMS summary within 5 minutes.**

The probate close rate at 6 months from a respectful Day 1 back-off is **dramatically higher** than the close rate from a Day 1 push. Patience compounds.

## 3.3 Probate Four Options (when they're ready to talk)

> "When families are working through probate, there are usually four paths for the property:
>
> One — sell to a cash buyer like us, as-is. Estate gets clean cash, easy to distribute among heirs, closes in 14-21 days once court authority is in place.
>
> Two — list with a real estate agent and go for top retail dollar. Takes longer, more showings, but if the property's in good shape it sometimes nets more.
>
> Three — fix it up and sell retail, if it needs work and you've got the bandwidth. Higher payout, more headache.
>
> Four — keep it. Some families turn the property into a rental, or one heir buys out the others.
>
> No wrong answer here — depends on the family, the timeline, the property condition. Want me to walk through which one usually fits which situation?"

## 3.4 Probate UMBC Listening Path

- **U:** Has the court issued Letters of Authority? Are there any deadlines? Any urgency from heirs?
- **M:** Are they the only PR or are there siblings/heirs? Is the property a burden (vacant, decaying, costing money)? What would they do with cash today?
- **B:** Do they have a sense of what the property's worth? Have they had it appraised for the estate? Anyone else made an offer yet?
- **C:** Is anyone living there, or is it vacant? Any major issues? When was it last updated? Personal property still inside?

## 3.5 Common Probate Objections

### "I need to talk to my siblings first"

This is **not** an objection — it's the truth in probate. Treat it as a real next step.

> "Of course — that absolutely makes sense, this is a family decision. Let me make it easier for you when you talk to them: I'll text you a one-page summary right after we hang up — what we'd typically offer for an estate property in this kind of shape, what the timeline looks like, what comes off the top. That way they're seeing the same info you are. After you've had that conversation, what's a reasonable time for me to circle back? End of next week? Two weeks?"

### "We don't even know what to do with the property yet"

> "Totally normal — most families I talk to are in that exact spot for the first month or two. Want me to send over a one-pager that covers the four options, just so you've got it when the family's ready to discuss? No follow-up calls until you say so."

### "We've already got an offer from someone else"

> "Got it — what's their timeline and structure? [Listen.] Okay. Without knocking what they're doing, here's what I'd offer — Danielle can run the numbers from our end in a 10-minute call, and you'll have a real second number to compare against. Worst case you confirm the first offer's good. Best case you find out you can do better. Either way it's free information. Want me to set that up?"

**Never trash-talk a competing offer. Position as "second opinion = free information."**

## 3.6 Probate Closing TARP (qualified)

Same shape as LP, but with explicit acknowledgement of the family-decision dimension:

> "[first_name] — what I'd suggest is a 15-minute call with Danielle. She'll go through the property in detail, talk through how we'd structure the sale through the estate, and by the end you'll have a real number to take back to your siblings. No pressure on that call either. Does [day] at [time] work, or [backup]?"

## 3.7 Probate Voicemail

> "Hi [first_name], this is Kareem with Wright Home Offer. I'm sorry for your loss. I'm calling about [decedent_name]'s estate and [property_address] — when your family is ready, I'd love to walk through the four options that usually exist. There is zero rush from my end. My number's [number]. Take care."

# Section 4 — Redemption Window (Post-Sheriff-Sale)

**Situation:** Sheriff sale has happened. Homeowner has a redemption window — typically 0-30 days for mortgage foreclosure, up to 30 days post-confirmation for tax foreclosure. **Most don't know this is possible.** Distress states are usually **learned helplessness (#1.3)** or **shame (#1.1)** — they think they've already lost.

**Tone:** Educational, helpful, hopeful. **They've been told they lost. We're the messenger of "wait, you might still have a path."**

## 4.1 RW Phone Opener

> "Hi, is this [first_name]? Kareem with Wright Home Offer in [county] County.
>
> I know [property_address] sold at sheriff sale on [auction_date]. The reason I'm calling — most people in your situation don't realize that in Ohio, you usually still have a redemption window after the sale. That means there's still a path where you walk away with money instead of nothing. I'm not promising anything — depends on your case — but it's worth five minutes to walk through it. Got the time?"

**The hook is "walk away with money instead of nothing."** That's the load-bearing phrase. State it calmly, not as a pitch.

## 4.2 RW Four Options

> "When the sheriff sale's already happened and you're in the redemption window, there are usually four paths:
>
> One — fund the redemption and sell to us. We provide the cash to redeem the property from the sale buyer, then we buy it from you at an agreed price. You walk away with money, the property closes, you're done.
>
> Two — assign your redemption rights to us. Even simpler — you transfer the right to redeem, we redeem ourselves, you get paid for that transfer. Faster cash to you, less paperwork.
>
> Three — emergency Chapter 13 bankruptcy. Sometimes extends the redemption window or restructures the debt. Talk to an attorney for that one.
>
> And four — let it ride. The redemption window expires, the sale buyer keeps the property, you get nothing. That's the default if nobody acts.
>
> Which of those sounds worth digging into?"

## 4.3 RW UMBC Listening Path

- **U:** When does the redemption window expire? (Always verify on county docket — many homeowners don't know.) Is there a confirmation hearing scheduled?
- **M:** Why did they end up here — financial, emotional, mental? Are they angry at the bank/sale buyer? Resigned? Hopeful?
- **B:** What did the property sell for at auction? Do they know what it's likely worth fixed up? What would they want to walk away with?
- **C:** Is anyone still living there? Any major issues?

## 4.4 Common RW Objections

### "Nobody told me I had a redemption window"

This is the **most common** RW response. Lean into it.

> "Yeah, you're not alone — it's wild how often this gets missed. The bank doesn't tell you, the sheriff doesn't tell you, sometimes even your own attorney doesn't bring it up. The window's real, it's in Ohio Revised Code, and it's running right now. Let me walk you through what it looks like and what your options are. Five minutes."

### "Is this even legal?"

> "Totally legal — Ohio Revised Code 5721 covers tax foreclosure redemption, and mortgage redemption is part of the standard foreclosure process. We do this with attorneys involved on both sides; there's no gray area. If it would help, I can put you in touch with our title attorney before we'd ever sign anything — you can verify it directly."

### "How much would I get?"

> "Depends on what the property's worth, what the sale price was, and what the redemption math looks like. Honestly, I can't give you a number — that's Danielle's call. Want me to set up a 15-minute call with her? She'll run the math and you'll have a real number."

## 4.5 RW Closing TARP (qualified)

Same shape, with explicit deadline anchoring:

> "Your redemption window's got [X] days left — we don't have a lot of room here. Let's get Danielle on with you tomorrow. She'll spend 15 minutes on the math — what the property's worth, what the redemption costs, what you'd walk with. Does [tomorrow] at [time] work, or [backup]?"

## 4.6 RW Voicemail

> "Hi [first_name], Kareem with Wright Home Offer. I'm calling about [property_address] — I know it sold at sheriff sale, and I want to make sure you know about Ohio's redemption window. Most people don't realize this exists. It usually means you can still walk away with money instead of nothing. My number's [number]. Time-sensitive — please call me back today if you can. Take care."

# Section 5 — Universal Branches (any preset)

## 5.1 If they ask "How did you get my number?"

> "Public records. The [foreclosure case / probate filing / sheriff sale notice] in [county] County is part of the public docket — I'm a local Ohio buyer and I work this market every day. Totally up to you whether to keep talking. If you want, I can text you the public-records link before we go any further."

## 5.2 If they ask "Are you a wholesaler?"

> "No — I'm with Wright Home Offer, we're a local Ohio company. When we close on a deal, we close on it ourselves. We don't assign contracts, we don't flip paperwork, we're not a national chain. I can text you our address and license info if it'd help."

## 5.3 If they say "Send me something in writing"

> "Will do. Texting you a one-page summary right now — the four options I just walked through, my contact info, and a quick FAQ. If anything in there raises questions, just reply to that thread. Talk soon."

**Tag `info_sent` in DataSift. Send the SMS within 5 minutes while top-of-mind.**

## 5.4 If they go non-committal — "Sure, maybe, let me think"

Use the **Quick Interrupt** from [ISA-SALES-FRAMEWORK.md §6](ISA-SALES-FRAMEWORK.md):

> "You think you'll [whatever]? I might be a little crazy, but it sounds like you may not be 100% certain you want to [sell / move forward / talk to Danielle] — and that's okay."

If non-committal persists → run the 5-Step Recovery (Framework §6).

## 5.5 If they go vague — "I need to think about it"

Run **Boxing Objections** in exact order ([ISA-SALES-FRAMEWORK.md §7](ISA-SALES-FRAMEWORK.md)):

1. "Is it the idea of working with me? Did I do something wrong?"
2. "Is it the process?"
3. "Is it the price?"
4. "Are you considering other **options**?" (never "offers")

## 5.6 If they say "Not interested" — Verify The No

Always:

> "Okay — so you're not interested in a cash offer for the property?"

If still no:

> "So what has changed?"

If still no:

> "Do you know someone who might be interested in a cash offer?"

If still no:

> "That's okay. I appreciate the time. If anything changes — [number]. Take care."

**Re-status the lead. Never tag Dead. Schedule the appropriate recycle (see [ISA-CADENCE.md](ISA-CADENCE.md)).**

# Section 6 — Voicemail Discipline

Every dial that hits voicemail gets a tested VM. **Voicemails are free retargeting.**

Three rules:

1. **15 seconds max.** Long VMs get deleted.
2. **State the property address.** Confirms you're not a robocaller and gives them something concrete.
3. **State the number twice.** People miss it the first time.

VM templates are in each preset section above. Rotate Day 1 / Day 2 / Day 3 variants so the same prospect doesn't hear the same message four times in a row.
