# ISA Onboarding — Kareem's Training & Ramp Plan (Week 1 to Week 4)

**Audience:** Kareem (the new ISA), Aaron (manager), Mike (co-trainer).
**Purpose:** A robust, interactive training plan for Kareem's first four weeks. Reading the SOPs is not enough — this doc layers training videos, daily checkpoints, written quizzes, practical roleplays, and Mike+Kareem collaboration on top of the SOP reading so Kareem can demonstrate competence, not just exposure.

**Companion doc:** [ISA-ONBOARDING-WORKBOOK.md](ISA-ONBOARDING-WORKBOOK.md) — the fillable quiz pages. Print and hand to Kareem each morning. Aaron + Mike grade.

---

## The KPI Kareem is being trained against

| Metric | Target | Why this number |
|---|---|---|
| **Signed contracts/month** | **4** | The North Star — what success looks like |
| **Set appointments/week** | **7-10** | Floor for hitting 4 contracts at industry-standard 9-14% appt-to-contract conversion |
| Appointments/day cruise | 1.5-2 | Reverse-math: 7-10/week ÷ 5 working days |
| Daily dials cruise | 100-120 | Across both FTM and Bulk Top-250 queues |

**Onboarding is over** when Kareem hits 7+ set appointments in a single week, sustainably. Target = end of Week 4.

## What gets coached vs. corrected

**Coached** (improvement areas — addressed via roleplay + audited calls):
- Tonality (rushed, scripted-sounding, eager)
- TARP fluency (the words flow, don't get read off the page)
- UMBC depth (Socratic follow-ups, not face-value motivation)
- Boxing Objection sequence speed
- Silence control after calibrated questions
- Mike+Kareem handshake quality

**Corrected** (immediate, non-negotiable — same-day intervention):
- Cadence misses (>24h overdue callback)
- Data hygiene (missing tags, blank notes)
- Off-script in a damaging way (price quoted, "other offers" said, identifying as wholesaler)
- Buy-box violations (handing off a lead that doesn't fit our criteria)
- Skipping the daily group-text touchpoints

## Smrtphone-specific things Kareem needs to know

- **Local presence dialing** is on by default. Outbound calls show a local Ohio number to maximize answer rate.
- **Voicemail drop** is enabled — Kareem can pre-load a 15-sec VM per preset and trigger it during power-dial without speaking.
- **Call recording** is on for every outbound call. Kareem should not say anything on a call he wouldn't want Aaron, Danielle, Mike, or a TCPA auditor to hear.
- **DNC list** is auto-synced from DataSift `do_not_contact` tag. Kareem still verifies before any dial — manual STOP requests during a call get tagged immediately.
- **Power-dial line count:** 3-line default during dial blocks; drop to 1-line for probate Day 1 (never power-dial a grieving family).
- **Live-listen** — Aaron and Mike can join Kareem's calls in listen-only mode for coaching. Used during Week 1-2.

## Things Kareem should NOT do (common new-rep mistakes)

- **Don't quote a price.** Ever. Even hypothetically. Same-Side framing requires zero pricing authority.
- **Don't argue with denial.** Parallel-track instead.
- **Don't push a probate lead in active grief.** Back off, recycle, send the SMS summary, wait.
- **Don't tag Dead.** Re-status only. Every "no" gets verified.
- **Don't fill silence.** After a calibrated question, after the offer's been mentioned, after a label — wait. 4 seconds minimum. 8-10 is better.
- **Don't say "other offers."** Always "other options."
- **Don't identify as a wholesaler.** Wright Home Offer closes on its own contracts.
- **Don't skip Permission-to-say-no.** It's the load-bearing piece of TARP.
- **Don't skip Mike+Kareem touchpoints.** The tag handshake breaks down without the dialogue.
- **Don't dial a record without checking tags.** If `mike_active_sms` is on it, skip.
